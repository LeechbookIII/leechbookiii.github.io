Old English
Modern English
gongewifran bite.
spider bite.
xxvi. Wiþ cancre.
xxvi. For cancer.
xxxvii. Wiþ þam þe wif ne mæge bearn acennan; and gif of wife nelle gan æfter þam beorþre þæt gecyndelic sie; and gif of wife sie dead bearn; and wiþ þam gif wif blede to swiþe æfter þam beorþre.
xxxvii. In case a woman cannot conceive a child; and if after the birth the natural [thing] does not go from the woman;10 and if a dead child is in a woman; and in case a woman bleeds too much after the birth.
xxxviii. Wiþ þam þe wifum sie forstanden hira monað gecynd and wiþ þam gif wife to swiþe offlowe sio monoþ gecynd.
xxxviii. In case a woman's monthly cycle is hindered and in case a woman's the monthly cycle flows too heavily.
xxxviiii. Wiþ smeagea wyrme: smiring and anlegen and beþing and sealf.
xxxviiii. For a penetrating worm: an ointment and a laying11 and an ointment and a salve.
xL. Wiþ þam þe man sie monaþ seoc.
xL. In case a person is month sick.12
xLi. Wiþ ealle feondes costunga: drenc and sealf; wiþ þon ilcan and hu man scyle gewitseocne man lacnian; and hu mon scyle wyrcean spiw drenc utyrnendum.
xLi. Against all temptations of the devil: a drink and a salve; against the same and how a person should cure the wit-sick person; and how a person should make a spew drink for diarrhea.
xLii. Wiþ þam gif swiþ drenc on men gesittan and he nelle utgan.
xLii. In case a strong drink settles on a person and it will not go out.
xLiii. Wiþ attres drence.
xLiii. For a drink of poison.
xLiiii. Wiþ lusum.
xLiiii. For lice.
xLv. Wiþ þam gif þorn stinge mon on fot oððe hreod and þonne nelle ofgan.
xLv. In case a thorn or a reed stings a person on the foot and then will not come out.
xLvi. Wiþ æsmælum and wiþ eallum eagna wærce.
xLvi. For æsmæl13 and against all eye pain.
xLvii. Wiþ lyft adle, gif sie muþ woh oððe won: læcedom and beþing and bæþ sealf and leah and blodes læs.
xLvii. For paralysis, if the mouth is bent or bruised: a leechdom and an ointment and a bath [and] a salve and a ley and a blood letting.
xLviii. Wiþ ficadle: drenc and beþing.
xLviii. For fig disease: a drink and an ointment.
xLviiii. Wiþ sculdor wærce and earma.
xLviiii. For shoulder and arm pain.
L. Wiþ cneowa sare.
L. For sore knees.
