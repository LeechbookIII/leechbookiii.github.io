xxvi. Wiþ cancre.
xxvi. Against cancer.
xxxvii, Wiþ þam þe wif ne mæge bearn acennan and gif of wife nelle gan æfter þam beorþre þæt gecyndelic sie and gif of wife sie dead bearn and wiþ þam gif wif blede to swiþe æfter þam beorþre.
xxxvii. Against when a woman cannot produce a child and if after the birth the natural [thing] does not go from the woman13 and if a dead child is from the woman and against if the woman bleeds to much after the birth.
xxxviii. Wiþ þam þe wifum sie forstanden hira monað gecynd and wiþ þam gif wife to swiþe off lowe sio monoþ gecynd.
xxxviii. Against when their monthly nature is hindered for women and against if the monthly nature of a woman flows too quickly in her monthly nature.
xxxviiii . Wiþ smeagea wyrme smiring and an legen and beþing and sealf.
xxxviiii. Against the penetrating worm a spread and a laying14 and an ointment and a salve.
xL. Wiþ þam þe man sie monaþ seoc.
xL. Against when a person is month-sick.
xLi. Wiþ ealle feondes costunga drenc and sealf. wiþ þon ilcan and hu man scyle gewitseocne man lacnian and hu mon scyle wyrcean spiw drenc utyrnendum.
xLi. Against all temptations of the devil, a drink and a salve; against the same and how a person should cure the wit-sick person and how a person should make a purgative spew-drink.
xLii. Wiþ þam gif swiþ drenc on men gesittan and he nelle ut gan.
xLii. Against if a strong drink settles on a person and it will not go out.
xLiii. Wiþ attres drence.
xLiii. Against a drink of poison.
xLiiii. Wiþ lusum.
xLiiii. Against lice.
xLv. Wiþ þam gif þorn stinge mon on fot oððe hreod and þonne nelle ofgan.
xLv. Against if a thorn or a reed stings a person on the foot and then will not go out.
xLvi. Wiþ æsmælum and wiþ eallum eagna wærce.
xLvi. Against contractions of the eye15 and against all eye pain.
xLvii. Wiþ lyftadle gif sie muþ woh oððe won læcedom and beþing and bæþ sealf and leah and blodes læs.
xLvii. Against paralysis, if the mouth is crooked or not straight, a leechdom and an ointment and a bath [and] a salve and a lye and blood-letting.
xLviii. Wiþ ficadle drenc and beþing.
xLviii. Against fig disease, a drink and an ointment.
xLviiii. Wiþ sculdor wærce and earma.
xLviiii. Against shoulder and arm pain.
