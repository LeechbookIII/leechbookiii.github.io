127r

Old English
Modern English
of þam wyrtum þonne þu hie ofgoten hæbbe.
over the herbs when you have poured over them.
Lxviiii. Gif man sie maga afurod and forþunden genim holen leafa micle twa hand fulla gescearfa swiþe male wyl on meolcum oþþæt hie syn wel mearuwe usla snæd mælum ete þonne .vi. snæda; on morgen .iii. and on æfen .iii. and æfter his mete do þus .viiii. niht leng gif him þearf sie.
Lxviiii. If stomach of a person is sour and swollen, take two large handfuls of holly leaves. Cut very small. Boil in milk until they are very soft. Pick out a bit at a time. Then eat six morsels, three in the morning and three at night, and after his food do thus seven nights long if his need be.
Gif mon biþ aþunden ete rudan and drince he biþ hal.
If person is swollen, eat pennyroyal and drink [it]. He is whole.
Wiþ magan wærce rudan sæd and cwic seolfor and eced berge on neaht nestig.
Against stomach pain, taste pennyroyal seed and quicksilver and vinegar after a night's fast.
Eft gnid on eced and on wæter polleian sele drincan sona þæt sar toght.
Afterwards, grind pennyroyal in vinegar and in water. Give to drink. Soon the soreness goes away.
LXX. Wiþ wambe wærce ofgeot polleian and drince and sume binde to þam nafolan and wite georne þæt sio wyrt awg ne aglide sona biþ sel.
LXX. Against womb pain, pour over pennyroyal and drink [it] and bind some to the navel and know earnestly that the herb will not glide away. Soon it is well.
Wiþ magan wærce wuduþistles þone grenan mearhþe biþ on þam heafde sele him etan mid hatan ele.
Against stomach pain, give the green marrow that is in the head of a wood thistle to him to eat with hot oil.
Wiþ wambe heardnesse geclænsa giþcorn gnid on ceald wæter sele him drincan.
Against womb hardness, cleanse caper spurge. Grind in cold water. Give [it] to him to drink.
Lxxi. Wiþ springe gnid saluian wit hunig smire mid sona biþ sel.
Lxxi. Against an ulcer, grind common sage with honey. Smear [the ulcer] with [it]. Soon it is well.
Eft wyrc sealfe nim hand fulle spring wyrte and hand fulle wegbrædan and hand fulle magþan and hand fulle niðewearde doccan
Afterwards, make a salve. Take a handful of caper spurge and a handful of greater plantain and a handful of chamomile and a handful of the lower part of yellow pond lily
