returns to you, you should not say any word to himbefore you arrive at the plant that you previously marked in the evening. Then sing Benedicte and Pater Noster and litanies. Dig up the plant. Let that short knife pierce [it] therein. Go afterwards as early as you can to the church and lay [the plant] under the altar with that short knife. Let [them] lie [there] until the sun is up. Wash afterwards. Put [it] into a drink, with marsh mallow and lichen from a crucifix. Boil three times in milk. Pour holy water on [it] three times. Sing Pater Noster and credos and Gloria in Excelsis Deo on [it] and sing litanies on it and also write across it on all four sides with a sword in the [figure of] a cross and drink that drink afterwards. Soon he is well.

Afterwards for that, lay these plants under an altar [and] let sing nine masses over [them]: frankincense, holy salt, three heads of garlic, the low hanging part of bittersweet nightshade, and elecampane. Take a cupful of milk in the morning. Drip holy water into [it] three times. Sup it, hot, as often as he can. Eat with three morsels of bittersweet nightshade and when he wants to rest, have coals therein. Lay frankincense and bittersweet nightshade on the coals and smoke him with [that] so that he sweats and smoke that house entirely and eagerly make the sign of the cross on that person and when he goes in to rest, [have him] eat three morsels of elecampane.
