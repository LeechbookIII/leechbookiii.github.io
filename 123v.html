Modern English
oþþe nihtgengan smire his andwlitan mid þis se sealfe and on his eagan do and þær him lichoma sar sie and recelsa hine and sena gelome his þing biþ sona selre.
or night-goer seizes a man, smear his face with this salve and do [so] on his eyes and where his body is sore and cense him and sign frequently. Soon his thing is better.
Lxii. Wið ælfadle nim bisceopwyrt. finul. elehtre. ælfþonan nioþowearde. and gehalgodes cristes mæles ragu and stor do ælcre hand fulle bebind ealle þa wyrta on claþe bedyp on font wætre gehalgodum þriwa læt singan ofer .iii. mæssan ane omnibus sanctis oþre contra tribulationem þriddan pro infirmis.
Lxii. Against elf disease, take bishopwort, fennel, lupine, the lower part of bittersweet nightshade and the lichen of a holy crucifix and frankincense. Take a handful of each. Bind all the plants in a cloth. Dip [them] into a fountain of holy water three times. Let three masses be sung over them: one Omnibus Sanctis, another Contra Tribulationem, a third Pro Infirmis.
Do þonne gleda angled fæt and lege þa wyrta on gerec þone man mid þa wyrtum ær undern and on niht and sing letania and credan and pater noster and writ him cristes mæl on ælcum lime and nim lytle hand fulle þæs ilcan cynnes wyrta gelice gehalgode; and wyl on meolce dryp þriwa gehalgodes wætres on and supe ær his mete him biþ sona sel.
Put then hot coals in a chafing dish and lay those plants in [it]. Smoke that person with the plants before 9 a.m. and at night and sing litanies and credos and Pater Noster and write on each of his limbs the sign of the cross and take a little handful of the same plants of that kind, likewise consecrated, and boil in milk. Drip three [drops] of the holy water into [it] and sup [it] before his food. Soon he is well.
Wiþ þon ilcan gang on þunres æfer þonne sunne on setle þær þu wite elenan standan sing þonne benedicte and pater noster and letanian and sting þin seax on þa wyrte læt stician þær on gang þe aweg gang eft to þonne dæg and niht furþum scade on þam ilcan uhte gang ærest to ciricean and þe gesena and gode þe bebeod gang þonne swigende and þeah þe hwæt hwega egeslices
Against that same condition, on Wednesday evening, when the sun is in its seat, where you should know elecampane stands, then sing Benedicte and Pater Noster and litanies and thrust a short sword into those plants. Let [it] pierce there in. Go away from [there]. Go afterwards to [that place] when day and night just divide at that same dawn. Go first to the church and bless yourself and ground yourself to God. Go then in silence and although a somewhat fearful thing
