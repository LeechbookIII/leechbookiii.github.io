The person who puts a portion of fox grease on the eyes for about thirty nights, he is always whole.
Gif mist sie fore eagum, nim cildes hlond and huniges tear meng tosomne begea emfela. smire mid þa eagan innan.
If darkness is before the eyes, take the urine of a child and a tear-like drop of honey. Mix them both together equally. Smear into the eyes with [it]22.
Eft, hrefnes geallan and leaxes and eles and feld beon hunig meng tosomne. Smire mid þære sealfe innan þa eagan.
Afterwards, mix together the bile of a crab and the bile of a salmon and the bile of a lamprey and field bee honey. Smear inside the eyes with that salve. Against the white speck, grind burned salt and beetle nut and poison to dust, all equally, and put on the eyes. Wash lightly with boiling water and smear afterwards with the milk of a woman.
Wiþ flie, gebærned sealt and swegles æppel and attrum ealra emfela gnid to duste and do on þa eagan. Þweah leohtlice mid wylle wætre and smire æfter mid wifes meolce.
If worms are painful in the eyes, inside the eyelids, put the sap of celandine on the painful [things]. The worms are dead and the eyes whole.
Gif wyrmas sien on eagum scearpa, þa bræwas innan, do on þa scearwan celeþonian seap. Þa wyrmas bioþ deade and þa eagan hale.
If flesh grows on the eyes, [put] a press of wormwort on the eyes until he is well.
Gif flæsc on eagum weaxe, wring wyrmwyrte on þa eagan oþþæt him sel sie.
If a red spongy excrescence grows on the eyes, drip hot blood of a pigeon or blood of a swallow or milk of a woman on [the eyes] until the excrescence is gone.
