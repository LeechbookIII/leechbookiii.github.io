Old English
Modern English
nim sigel hweorfan and hwite clæfran wisan and wudurofan do on gode butran aseoh þurh clað and lacna siþþan.
take smooth cat's-ear and white clover sprouts and woodruff. Put in good butter. Strain through a cloth and heal afterwards.
Gif sio eaxl upstige nim do þa sealfe do hwon wearme mid feþere him bið sona sel
If the shoulder sticks up, take that salve. Put a little on warm with a feather. Soon he is well.
Wyrc godne dolh drenc nim agrimonian and wudurofan do on god ealo sele drincan godne scenc fulne on neaht nestig.
Make a good wound drink. Take agrimony and woodruff. Put in good ale. Give a good cup full to drink after a night's fast.
Gif gebrocen ban sie on heafde and of nelle cnua grene betonican and lege on þæt dolh gelome oþþæt þa ban of syn and þæt dolh gebatod.
If the broken bone is on the head and will not go off, pound green betony and lay [it] on that wound often until the bone is off and the wound is healed.
xxxiiii. Wiþ hundes slite cnuwa ribban lege on þæt dolh and rudan wyl on butran lacna mid þæt dolh.
xxxiiii. Against the bite of a hound, pound ribwort. Lay [it] on that wound and boil pennyroyal in butter. Heal that wound with [it].
Gif sinew syn forcorfene nim renwyrmas gecnuwa wel lege on; oþþæt him hale synd gif sinew sien gescruncene nime æmettan mid hiora bedgeride wyl on wætre and beþe mid and rece þa sinew geornlice.
If the sinew is cut, take intestinal worms. Grind them well. Lay [them] on it until they [the sinews are whole. If the sinew is shrunken take ants with their nest. Boil in water and warm [the sinews] with [it] and smoke the sinew diligently.
xxxv. Wiþ gongewifran bite nim lienne æg gnid on ealu hreaw and sceapes tord niwe swa he nyte sele him drincan godne scenc fulne.
xxxv. Against spider bite, take an egg of a hen and a fresh turd of a sheep. Grind them raw in ale so that he does not know. Give him a good cup full to drink.
xxxvi. Wiþ cancre nim gate geallan and hunig meng tosomne begea emfela do on þæt dolh.
xxxvi. Against cancer, take the bile of a goat and honey. Mix together, both in equal parts. Put on that wound.
