Modern English
blede to swiþe æfter þam beorþre nioþowearde cletan wyl on meolce sele etan and supan þæt wos.
bleed too quickly after the birth, boil the lower part of greater burdock in milk. Give the juice to eat and to eat.
xxxviii. Wiþ þon þe wifum sie forstanden hira monaþ gecynd wyl on ealað hleomoc and twa curmeallan sele drincan and beþe wif on hatum baþe and drince þone drenc on þam baþe hafa þe ær geworht clam of beor dræstan and of grenre mucgwyrte and merce and of berene melwe meng ealle tosomne gehrer on pannan clæm on þæt gecynde lim and on þone cwið nioþoweardne þonne hio of þam baðe gæþ and drince scenc fulne þæs ilcan scences wearmes and bewreoh þæt wif wel and læt beon swa beclæmed lange tide þæs dæges do swa tuwa swa þriwa swæþer þu scyle þu scealt simle þam wife bæþ wyrcean and drenc sellan on þa ilcan tid þe hire sio gecynd æt wære ahsa þæs æt þam wife.
xxxviii. Against when a woman's monthly cycle is delayed33, boil brooklime and two knapweeds in ale. Give to drink and bathe the woman in a hot bath and drink the drink in that bath. Have already prepared a poultice of beer dregs and of green mugwort and wild celery and of barley meal. Mix all together. Stir in a dish. Smear on that naturely part and on the lower part of the womb when she goes from the bath and drink a cupful of the same warm draught and clothe that woman well and let [her] remain smeared for a large part of the day. Do either two or three times, whichever you must. You shall always make a bath for that woman and give the drink at the same time that the nature next comes to her. Ask that the woman [about this].
Gif wife to swiþe offlowe sio monað gecynd genim niwe horses tord lege on hate gleda læt reocan siþe betweoh þa þeoh up under þæt hrægl þæt se mon swæte swiþe.
If her monthly nature flows too quickly from a woman, take a fresh dung of a horse. Lay [it] on a hot coal. Let [it] smoke strongly between the thighs, up under her clothes, so the person sweats a lot.
xxxviiii. Wið smeawyrme smiring nim swines geallan
xxxviiii. Against a penetrating worm, a spread. Take the bile of a swine
33. Cockayne: "In case mulieribus menstrual suppressa sunt..."
Olds: "In case a woman is stopped up in her monthly cycle..." ↩
