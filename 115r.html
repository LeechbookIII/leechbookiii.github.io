Old English
Modern English
Wyrc briw wiþ lungenadle. Nim betonican and marubian, wermod, hindheoloþan, wenwyrt nioþoweard, elehtre, elene, rædic, eoforþrote, feldmore. Gecnua ealle swite wel and wyl on buteran and awring þurh clað. Scead on þæt wos beren mela. Hrer on blede butan fyre oþþæt hit sie swa þicce swa briw. Ete .iii. snæda mid þy drence wearmes. Eft, wyl on hunige anum marubian. Do hwon beren mela to. Ete on neahtnestig and þonne þu him selle drenc oððe briw. Sele him hatne and læt gerestan þone man æfter tide dæges on þa swiðran sidan and hafa þone earm aþened.
Make a pottage against lung disease. Take betony and white horehound, wormwood, wood sage, the lower part of lesser celandine, lupin, garden radish, stemless carline thistle, wild carrot. Pound all very well and boil in butter and wring through a cloth. Scatter barley meal on that juice. Stir into a bowl [that is] against a flame until it is as thick as a pottage. Eat three morsels with a drink of the warm [pottage]. Afterwards, boil white horehound alone in honey. Put a little barley meal in [it]. Eat after a night's fast and then you should give him a drink or pottage. Give the hot [thing] to him and let the person rest after a day's time on the right side and have the arm extended.
xv. Wiþ magan wærce, wyl wic on cu meolce. Ado þæt wic of. Supe hwon wearm. Sona biþ sel.
xv. Against stomach pain, boil wych elm in cow milk. Take the wych elm out. Eat a little warm. Soon [it] is well.
Wiþ aþundenesse and man nelle myltan his mete, wyl on wætere polleian and leaccersan. Sele drincan. Him biþ sona sel.
xvi. Wiþ milte wærce, cnua grene sealh rinde. Seoð on hunige anum. Sele him etan .iii. snæda on neahtnestig.
Against swelling and the person who cannot digest his food, boil pennyroyal and garlic-mustard in water. Give to drink. Soon he is well.
xvii. Wiþ lenden wærce, marubie, nefte, bogen (emfela ealra). Do on god ealu. Wyrc to drence. Swet hwon. Sele drincan. Licge uppe and æfter þon gode hwile.
xvi. Against spleen pain, pound green willow bark. Boil alone in honey. Give him three morsels to eat after a night's fast.
xvii. Against loin pain, put white horehound, cat mint, ragwort (all in equal parts) in good ale. Make into a drink. Sweeten a little. Give to drink. Lie face up and [remain like this] afterwards for a good while.
