121v

Old English
Modern English
him biþ sona sel.
Soon he is well.
Eft nim cellendran adrig gewyrc to duste gemeng þæt dust wiþ wifes meoluc þe wæpned fede awring þurh hæwenne clað and smire þæt hale wonge mid and drype on þæt eare wærlice.
Afterwards, take dry celandine. Make [it] into a dust. Mix that dust with the milk of a woman who bore a male. Wring through blue cloth and smear the whole34 cheek with [it] and drip on the hurting ear.
Wyrc þonne beþinge genim brembel rinde and elm rinde. æsc rinde. slahþorn rinde. apuldor rinde. ifig rinde. ealle þas nioþowearde and hwerhwestan. smeruwyrt. eoforfearn. elene. ælfþone. betonice. marubie. redic. agrimonia. gescearfa þa wyrta on cetel and wyl swiðe þonne hit sie swiþe gewylled do of þam fyre and sete and gewyrc þam man setl ofer þam citele and bewreoh ðone man mid þæt se æþm ne mæge ut nahwær butan he mæge a geeþian bête hine mid þis se beþinge þa hwile þe he mæge aræfnan.
Then make an ointment. Take bramble bark and elm bark, ash bark, sloe thorn bark, crab-apple tree bark, ivy bark (all the lower part) and squirting cucumber, birthwort, polypody, elecampane, bittersweet nightshade, betony, white horehound, radish, agrimony. Cut the herbs into a kettle and boil vigorously. When it is thoroughly boiled, take [the kettle] off the fire and set [aside] and make the person sit over the kettle and cover the person so that the vapor may not escape out anywhere, unless he may breathe it [in]. Warm him with this ointment for the amount of time he may endure it.
Hafa him þonne oþer bæþ geara genim æmet bed mid ealle þara þe hwilum fleogað beoþ reade wyl on wætre beþe hine mid on gemet hatum.
Then have another bath ready for him. Take an ant bed with all those that sometimes fly [and] are red. Boil in water. Bathe him with [it] immensely hot.
Wyrc him þonne sealfe nim ælces þara cynnes wyrta wyl on buteran smire mid þa saran limu hie cwiciaþ sona.
Then make a salve for him. Take each of the herbs of that kind [mentioned above]. Boil in butter. Smear the sore limbs with [it]. They will come to life soon.
Wyrc him leage of ellen ahsan þweah his heafod mid colre him biþ sona bet and se man læte him blod ælce monþe on .vi. nihta ealdne monan and on fiftyne and on .xx.
Make a lay of elder ashes for him. Wash his head with [it] cool. Soon he is better and the man should let his blood each month during the seven-night-old moon and during fifteen[-night-old moon] and during twenty[-night-old moon].
