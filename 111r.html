Modern English
and tacnu hwæþer he libban mæg.
and signs [as to] whether he may live.
Lxvi. Drenc wiþ þam gif þeor sie on man.
Lxvi. A drink in case þeor18 is in a person.
Lxvii. Wiþ deofle seoce and wiþ deofle.
Lxvii. Against devil sickness and against the devil.
Lxviii. Wiþ wedenheorte: leoht drenc.
Lxviii. Against mad-heart: a light drink.
Lxviiii. Wiþ þam gif men sie maga asurod; and forþunden; and wiþ magan wærce; and gif man biþ aþunden.
Lxiiii. In case a person has a sour stomach; and against swelling; and against stomach pain; and if a person is swollen.
Lxx. Wiþ wambe wærce and wiþ magan wærce and wiþ wambe heardnesse.
Lxx. For womb pain and against stomach pain and against womb hardness.
Lxxi. Wið springe: smiring and sealf.
Lxxi. For ulcers: a spread and a salve.
Lxxii. Wiþ attre: drenc and smiring.
Lxxii. For poison: a drink and a spread.
Lxxiii. Wiþ þære geolwan adle.
Lxxiii. For the yellow disease.
Lxxiiii. Wiþ þam gif innelf si ute.
Lxxiv. In case the guts are out.
Lxxv. Wiþ ælcre innan untrymnesse and wiþ hefignesse and wiþ hleorblæce.
Lxxv. For every inner illness and against heaviness and against cheek blot.19
Lxxvi. Be þam hu man scyle halige sealfe wyrcean.
Lxxvi. About how a person should make a holy salve.
i. Wiþ þon þe mon on heafod ace: genim nioþowearde wrætte, do on readne wræd, binde þæt heafod mid.
i. In case a person aches in the head: take the lower part of madder, put [it] in a red bandage, bind the head with it. 
Wiþ þon ilcan: nim senepes sæd and rudan. Gegnid on ele. Do on hat wæter. Þweah gelome þæt heafod on þam wætre. He biþ hal.
For the same [ache]: take mustard seed and rue. Rub into oil. Put in hot water. Wash the head often in that water. He will be whole. 
Wiþ ealdum heafod ece: genim dweorgedwostlan, wyl on ele oððe on butran, smire mid þa þunwongan and bufan þam eagum on ufan þæt heafod. Þeah him sie gemynd oncyrred, he biþ hal.
For an old headache: take mountain rue. Boil in oil or in butter, smear the temples and above the eyes on the top of the head with it. Although his mind is perverted, he will be whole.20 
Wiþ swiþe ealdum heafod ece: nim sealt and rudan and ifig
Against a very old headache: take salt and mountain rue and an ivy
