Old English
Modern English
xvii. Wiþ linden wærce.
xvii. Against loin pain.
xviii. Wiþ wambe wærce and rysel wærce.
xviii. Against womb8 pain and abdomen pain.
xviiii. Wiþ blædder wærce.
xviiii. Against bladder pain.
xx. Wiþ þam gif man ne mæge gemigan and þam man þe stanas Weaxan on þære blæadran.
xx. Against if a person may not pass water and stones grow in the bladder of that person.
xxi. Wiþ þam gif man sie se utgang forseten.
xxi. Against if a person is constipated9.
xxii. Wiþ utsihtadle drenc and briw.
xxii. Against diarrhea, a drink and pottage.
xxiii. Wiþ þam wyrmum þe beoþ on mannes innoþe.
xxiii. Against the worms that are in someone's gut.
xxiiii. Wiþ lið wærce.
xxiiii. Against joint pain.
xxv. Wiþ weartum.
xxv. Against warts.
xxvi. Wið þam miclan lice smiring and bæþ and drenc and briw.
xxvi. Against the large body, a spread and a bath and a drink and a pottage.
xxvii. Wiþ singalum þurste untrumra manna.
xxvii. Against the continual thirst of sick people.
xxviii. Wiþ innan fortoge andsmæl þearma ece.
xxviii. Against inside colic and small intestine ache.
xxviiii. Wiþ þam þe man sie mid fyre anum forbærned and wiþ þam þe man sie mid wætan forbærned. and wiþ sunbryne.
xxviiii. Against if a person is burned only with fire; and against when a person is burned with liquid; and against sunburn.
xxx. Wiþ þeore drenc and eft wiþ þære and sceotendum wenne and eft be þing wiþ þam gif þeor gewunige on anre stowe.
xxx. Against þeor10, a drink; and also against that and against a shooting cyst and afterwards an ointment against that if þeor remains in one place.
xxxi. Wiþ wenne sealf.
xxxi. Against a cyst, a salve.
xxxii. Wiþ dolge sealf.
xxxii. Against a wound, a salve.
xxxiii. Wiþ þam gif man sie ufan on heafod wund and sie ban gebrocen and wiþ þam gif sio eaxl upstige and god dolh drenc and gif gebrocen ban sie on heafde and of nelle.
xxxiii. Against if someone is wounded above of the head and has a broken bone; and against if the shoulder protrudes11; and a good wound-drink and if the broken bone is in the head and will not go off.
xxxiiii. Wiþ hundes slite and wiþ þon gif sinew forcorfeneand wiþ þam gif sinew sien gescruncene.
xxxiiii. Against hound's bite and against if sinew is cut through and against if the sinew is shrunken.

8. Womb or stomach, belly ↩

11. Bosworth-Toller translates "upstige" as ascension or mounting. ↩
