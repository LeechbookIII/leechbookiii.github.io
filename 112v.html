112v

Old English
Modern English
and do on þa eagan siþþan
and put it on the eyes afterwards.
Wyrc gode drige scade-sealfe. Nim swegles æppel and gebærned sealt and pipor and attrum and hwit cwudu. Gegnid to duste. Asift þurh clað do lytlum on.
Make a good dry shed-salve. Take beetle nut and burned salt and pepper and with bile and mastic. Grind to dust. Sift through a cloth. Put a little on [him].
Eft, hwit cwudu and gebærned ofter syl gnid to duste and nytta swa þe þearf sie. Ægþer mæg adon flie of eagan.
Afterwards, grind mastic and burned oyster shell to dust and use as needed. Either may remove the white speck from the eyes.
Wyrc smeþe eag sealfe. Nim buteran, wyl on pannan. Afleot þæt fam of and ahlyttre þa buteran on blede. Do eft þæt hluttre on pannan. Gecnua celeþonian, bisceopwyrt, wudumerce. Wyl swiþe. Aseoh þurh clað. Nytta swa þe þearf sie.
Make a smooth eye salve. Take butter, boil it in a pan. Skim off the foam and clarify the butter in a bowl. Put afterwards that clarified [butter] in a pan. Pound celandine, marsh mallow [and] wild celery. Boil vigorously. Strain through a cloth. Use as needed.
III. Wiþ ear wærce, genim henne gelyndo and ofter scylle. Sete on gleda. Gewyrm hwon and dryp on þa earan. Sona beoð hale.
III. Against ear pain, take hen's grease and an oyster shell. Set on live coals. Warm a little and drip in the ears. Soon [they] are whole.
Eft, celendran seap and wifes meoluc gewyrm on scylle and dryp on þa earan.
Afterwards, warm the sap of coriander and the milk of a woman in the shell and drip in the ears.
Gif wyrmas sien on earan, do belenan seap wearm on þa wyrmas. Hie beoþ deade and feallað of and þa earan hale.
If worms are in the ears, put warm henbane sap on the worms. They will be dead and will fall off and the ears will be whole.
Eft, wring curmeallan seap on oþþe marubian oððe wermod wearmne. Sona him bið sel.
Afterwards, wring knapweed sap or white horehound or warm wormwood. Soon he is well.
Wyrc gode earsealfe. Genim bares geallan and fearres and ele, ealra emfela. Læt drypan wearn on þæt eare.
Make a good ear salve. Take the bile of a bear and the bile of a bull and oil, all in equal parts. Let drip the warm [salve] on that ear.
