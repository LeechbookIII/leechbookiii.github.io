110v

Old English
Modern English
Li. Wiþ fotasare.
Li. Against soreness of the feet.
Lii. Wiþ þam gif þu ne mæge bloddolg forwriþan.
Lii. Against if you cannot staunch a blood wound.
Liii. Wiþ þam gif meoloc sie gewerd.
Liii. Against if milk is spoiled.
Liii. Wiþ nihtgengean, sealf.
Liiii. Against night-goers, a salve.
Lv. Wiþ þam gif  man beo sio heafod panne gehlenced.
Lv. Against if the skull of a person is bent.
Lvi. Wið þam gif man men nelle meltan his mete.
Lvi. Against if a person cannot digest his food.
Lvii. Wiþ wif gemædlan.
Lvii. Against a woman's talk16.
Lviii. Wiþ feondes costunga.
Lviii. Against temptations of the devil.
Lviiii. Wiþ þeor wenne gif he sie man on cneowe oþþe on oþrum lime. 
Lviiii. Against þeor cyst if it is on the knee or another limb of a person.
Lx. Be þam hu mon scyle ear sealfe wyrcean. 
Lx. About how a person should make an ear salve.
Lxi. Wiþ ælf cynne, sealf, and wiþ nihtgengan and þam monnum þe deofol mid hæmð.
Lxi. Against elf-kind and against the night-goers and against those people who the devil had sex with, a salve.
Lxii. Wiþ ælfadle, læcedom, and eft hu mon sceal on þa wyrte singan ær hi mon nime and eft hu mon sceal þa wyrta don under weofod and ofer singan and eft tacnu be þam hwæþer hit sie ælf sogotha and tacn hu þu ongitan meaht hwæþer hine mon mæg gelacnian and drencas and gebedu wiþ ælcre feondes costunge.
Lxii. Against elf-illness, a leechdom, and afterwards how a person should sing on the plants before the person picks them and afterwards how someone should put those plants under the altar and sing over them and afterwards signs which [show] whether it is elf-sucking and a sign of how you might know whether that person may be cured and drinks and prayers against every temptation of the devil.
Lxiii. Tacnu hu þu meaht ongitan hwæþer mon sie on wæterælfadle and læcedom wiþ þam and gealdor on to singanne and þæt ilce mon mæg singan on wunda.
Lxiii. Signs [for] how you might know whether a person has water-elf-disease and a leechdom against that and a charm to sing on [it] and that same thing a person may sing on wounds.
Lxiiii. Wið deofle, liþe drenc, and ungemynde and wiþ deofles costunga.
Lxiiii. Against the devil and insanity17 and against temptations of the devil, a light drink.
Lxv. Wiþ þon gif mon sie gegymed
Lxv. Against if a person is cut
16. Possibly "madness, fury" ↩
17. confused, demented: Bosworth-Toller ↩
