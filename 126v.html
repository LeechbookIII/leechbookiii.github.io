126v

Old English
Modern English
forswelgan and be supe mid cu wearmum.
swallow it and eat it with warm cow.
Lxvi. Drenc gif þeor sie on man nim þas wyrte nioþewearde finol. bisceopwyrt. æscþrotan. ealra emfela þissa twega mæst ufewearde rudan and betonican ofgeot mid hluttrum ealaþ and gesinge .iii. mæssan ofer drince ymb .ii. niht þæs þe he ofgoten sie ær his mete and æfter.
Lxvi. A drink if þeor is in a person. Take these herbs: the lower part of fennel, bishopwort, vervain, all in equal parts. Of these two [take the] most: the upper part of pennyroyal and betony. Pour with pure ale and sing three masses over [it]. Drink about two nights from when it was poured, before his food and after.
Lxvii. Wiþ deofle seoce do on halig wæter and on eala bisceopwyrt. hindhiolaþan. agrimonian. Alexandrian. gyþrifan sele him drincan.
Lxvii. Against devil-sickness, put bishopwort, hindheal, agrimony, alexanders, corncocke in holy water and in ale. Give [it] to him to drink.
Eft cassuc. þefanþorn. stan crop. elehtre. finul. eoforþrote. cropleac of geot gelice.
Afterwards, pour [that drink] over reed, buckthorn, stone crop, lupine, fennel, stemless carline thistle, leek similarly.
Eft spiwe drenc wið deofle nim micle handfulle secges and glædenan do on pannan geot micelne bollan fulne ealaþ on bewyl healf gegnid .xx. lybcorna do on þæt þis is god drenc wiþ deofle;
Afterwards, [make] a spew drink against the devil. Take a large handful of sedge and yellow iris. Put in a pan. Pour a large, full bowl of ale into [it]. Boil to half. Grind twenty caper spurge fruits. Put in that [drink]. This is a good drink against the devil.
Lxviii. Leoht drenc wiþ weden heorte elehtre. bisceopwyrt. ælfþone. elene. cropleac. hindhioloþe. ontre. clate. Nim þas wyrta þonne dæg and niht scade sing ærest on ciricean letania and credan and pater noster gang mid þy sange to þam wyrtum ymbga hie þriwa ær þu hine nime and ga eft to ciricean gesing .xii. mæssan
Lxviii. A light drink against wooden heart: lupine, bishopwort, bittersweet nightshade, elecampane, leek, hindheal, garden radish, [and] greater burdock. Take those herbs when day and night divide. Sing first in a church litanies and the Creed and the Pater Noster. Walk with these songs to the herbs, go around them three times before you take them and go afterwards to church. Sing twelve masses
