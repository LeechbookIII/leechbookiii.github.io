109r

Old English
Modern English
i. Wiþ heafod ece and wiþ ealdum heafod ece and wiþ healfes heafodes ece.
i. Against a headache and against an old headache and against a half-head's ache.
ii. Wiþ aswollenum eagum and god eah sealf and wið miste on eagan and wið flie and wið wyrmum on eagum and wiþ þæt gif flæsc on eagum weaxe and gif on eagum weaxan reade sponge and gif eagan tyrenand sceade sealf to eagum. andsmeþe eah sealf.
ii. Against swollen eyes; and a good eye-salve; and against darkness in the eyes and against the white speck1 and against worms in the eyes and against that if flesh grows on the eyes and if a red spongy excrescence grows on the eyes and if eyes tear; and a shed-salve2 on the eyes; and a smooth eye salve.
iii. Wiþ earwærce and wiþ þæt gif wyrmas syn on earan and god ear sealf.
iii. Against an earache and against that if worms are in the ears and a good ear salve.
iiii. Wiþ toþ ece and gif teþ syn hole.
iiii. Against a toothache and if teeth are hollow3.
v. Wiþ innan tobrocenum muðe.
v. Against a mouth broken within.
vi. Wiþ ceocadle and wiþ ceol wærce.
vi. Against cheek disease and against throat pain.
vii. Wiþ heals wærce.
vii. Against a neck ache.
viii. Wiþ bite.
viii. Against cancer4.
viiii. Wiþ hwostan.
viiii. Against coughing.
x. Wiþ þam þe mon blode hræce.
x. Against when a person spits blood.
xi. Wiþ seondum geallan.
xi. Against oozing galls.
xii. Wiþ þære geolwanadle.
xii. Against that yellow disease5.
xiii. Wiþ breost wærce.
xiii. Against chest pain.
xiiii. Wiþ hwostan and wiþ lungenadle.
xiiii. Against cough and against lung disease.
xv. Wiþ magan wærce and wiþ aþundenesse.
xv. Against maw6 pain and against swelling7.
xvi. Wið milt wærce.
xvi. Against spleen pain.
1. White speck on the eye ↩
2. Sceadan: to scatter, shed. Implication is a powdered salve. ↩
3. Holian: to hollow out, dig, make a hole ↩
4. Literally "For bite," Bosworth-Toller cites the Leechdom "Wið canceradle, ðæt is, bite" as reason to believe "bite" refers to cancer in several leechdoms. ↩
5. Jaundice ↩
6. or stomach ↩
7. of the stomach or maw ↩
